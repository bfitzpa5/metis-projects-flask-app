Data Cleaning and Daily Commuters: Exploratory Analysis of New York Subway Traffic

For our first project, we were asked by a hypothetical non-profit, WomenTechWomenYes, to analyze MTA subway data. Their goal was to optimize the placement of our street teams, such that they could gather the most signatures, ideally from those who will attend their annual gala and contribute to our cause.

Background

I was put on a team with Alexei Demchouk, Jordan Leung and Marc Reich to design and implmenent this project. Our team took the approach that we should identify a small number of high-traffic stops and pick the right time to maximize impact at each stop. Additionally, we wanted to cross-reference this stops in areas with the most tech employers, specifically choosing times that target employees leaving work.

DataSet

p>We gathered the subway turnstile dataset from the MTA’s website. his data required a lot of cleaning.

About 98% of the data is in 4 hour blocks; however, there was also some turnstile data that was set in 1 hour blocks. We disgarded these stations as we had more then enough data and these 1 hour blocks were typically in smaller stations. We specifically zeroed in on late May/Early June, as the gala was at the beginning of the summer. Additionally, there were some turnstiles with negative or zero counts. We filtered these and extreme values though only including those within each station’s interquartile range.

To gather areas with the most tech employees, we scraped Built in NYC’s “50 NYC startups to watch in 2017”. After this, we went through the arduous process of getting the street addresses for each company and coverting those into longitude and latitude coordinates.

Analysis

Finding that many of the busy subway stations were already around many of the tech companies in NYC, our next objective was to identify the busiest subway times.

We filtered the subway turnstile data in six blocks:

  • Block 1 - 00:00 to 06:00
  • Block 2 - 04:00 to 10:00
  • Block 3 - 08:00 to 14:00
  • Block 4 - 12:00 to 18:00
  • Block 5 - 16:00 to 22:00
  • Block 6 - 20:00 to 02:00

Takeaways

  • Data Cleaning This project definitely showed us that data cleaning is a large part of being a data scientist. Most of our week was spent combing through the data and trying to make sure it was clean. This is a good lesson to learn in terms of budgeting time for future projects.

  • Working in a Team One effective measure our team took was to split work in a strategic manner. Towards the end of the project, I took a large part of the maps visualizings, Alexi and Jordan worked on the analysis, and Mark worked on the presentation. This enabled us to have a fuller project once everything came together.

  • Additional Improvements We definitely would love to expand this analysis, specifically by finding other ways to rank each stop/time block combo. We could do this by using a distance metric to rank by proximity to companies. We could also look at different days of week. Finally, we could create a weighted score of these ranking methods.
